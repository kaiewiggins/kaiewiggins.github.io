Whirlwind news drop for you! I'll start with the time-sensitive items:

Underworld Summer Camp & Library Visits, South Korea

I'm teaching a week-long "Underworld"-themed creative writing sleepaway camp for kids and teens next week! (OBVIOUSLY, we're gonna weave K-Pop Demon Hunters references in, but also check out our assigned reading, Misadventures In Ghosthunting by Melissa Yue, which draws on a Cantonese-Chinese talismans and traditions for a cool take on heritage, hauntings, and underworlds.)

We've come up with this super fun Haunted Hotel Tour activity. Teams will design "tour companies" with flags and costumes, map out the hotel/conference centre, and dream up stories of monsters and magic (and maybe aliens?) for various locations over the course of the week, before guiding the tours based on the final night!

I believe registrations are still open, for anyone interested (details on poster!)

After, I'll be doing author visits around Seoul, including a presentation+activity at Songpa Children's English Library (ages 10-13, 11 am, July 29, waitlist now full, sorry!), and a joint "Summer Night Readings" presentation+activity with Lee Edward Födi at Wiryeseong Library (ages 7-12, 6:30pm, July 30, register here!


Kids/Teen Writing Contest

View this post on Instagram

A post shared by Embassy of Canada to Korea (@canadakorea)

Also (potentially) of interest, I'm one of the judges for this kids & youth writing contest in celebration of the 2024-25 Canada-Korea Year of Cultural Exchanges and organized by the Korea Herald, Embassy of Canada to Korea, and the Creative Writing for Children Society.

There are various age brackets and story themes to choose from (highlighting Korean-Canadian history, icons, and cultural identity). The contest page is in Korean, but stories should be in English.

The submission deadline is August 7. Good luck to all the kids and teens competing this summer!


First Serial Fiction Release

I’m delighted that The Daily Tomorrow picked up my sentient geology eco-apocalypse weird/soft SF short Geosapiology: a brief history of geogenesis, the Stones, and the Theory of Appeasement (releasing as a seven-part serial from Jul. 6-12).
